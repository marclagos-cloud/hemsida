Who I am

About me

Hi! I'm Ingrid — a Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) working one-to-one with autistic children in the Stockholm area, across homes, schools and clinic settings. I work in both English and Swedish.

I hold a Master's in Education with a specialization in Applied Behavior Analysis from Vanderbilt University, where I also worked as a research assistant in an ABA research lab and in multi-sensory neuroscience research with autistic individuals. Today, alongside my clinical work, I'm pursuing a PhD in medical science at the Center of Neurodevelopmental Disorders (KIND) at Karolinska Institutet, within autism research.

Across two years in ABA clinics and schools — and four years in autism research with both young children and older kids — I've had the privilege of getting to know many, many different autistic children. If there's one thing they've taught me, it's that no two are alike. That's why nothing I do follows a template.

And the most important thing I believe: your child is not a problem to be fixed. My job is to work alongside them — so they feel safe, understood, and free to grow as exactly who they are.

My approach

About ABA — honestly

If you've read about ABA online, you may have come across criticism of it. I want you to know that I've read it too, and I take it seriously.

My work is neurodiversity-affirming and assent-based. In practice, that means I follow your child's lead, honor all the ways they communicate, and never use methods that push a child to simply comply. The goal is never to make your child "less autistic" or to teach them to mask who they are.

The goal is well-being: more ways to communicate, more autonomy, more play, more good days — as defined by your child and your family. If something doesn't feel right for your child, we change it.

How it works

  1. We start with a conversation

    You tell me about your child and your situation; I tell you how I work. Free, and no strings attached.

  2. Getting to know your child

    I take time to get to know your child — their strengths, interests and needs — together with you, at your child's pace.

  3. A plan for your family

    We set goals that feel meaningful to your family and your child, and shape a program around them — not the other way around.

  4. Sessions and follow-up

    Regular one-to-one sessions, ongoing support for you as parents, and continuous follow-up to make sure what we're doing is working — and feels good for your child.
